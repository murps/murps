MURPS

Minimalist Universal Roleplaying System

What you will need

Players

Any group of willing people looking to embark on an epic journey through creative storytelling. First-timers welcome.

Narrator

Someone to help guide and progress the story. They also resolve any disputes, and roleplay all the non-player characters.

Six-sided dice

Cubes are the most abundant platonic solids around. Scavenge them from other games or pick some up from a local game shop.

Pencil & Paper

Even the most seasoned adventurers know you can't keep track of all the important details in your head. Why should you?

Player Characters

Name

From personality to ancestry, a name can say a lot about a character. Try to embed your character's tone into their name.

Background

What life experiences shaped your character? Describe any events that led them to their current state. Where are they headed?

Skills

Describe your character in three sentences, each outlining a skill. Make one heroic and either two Novice or one Veteran and one Inept

Proficiency

Skills are given a degree of proficiency to determine the type of skill check needed in order to complete tasks at varying levels of dificulty.

Skill Checks

Degrees of Proficiency

  1. Inept— trouble with fundamental concepts
  2. Novice— most common among general population
  3. Veteran— established career
  4. Heroic— proven among peers
  5. Master— the best around
  6. Legendary— unheard of abilities

Degrees of Difficulty

  1. Easy— simple task
  2. Normal— basic complexity
  3. Challenging— increased complexity
  4. Hard— too complex for most people
  5. Rigorous— extreme focus and attention required to accomplish
  6. Near Impossible— nearing the limit of comprehension

Check Types

  1. Automatic Failure
  2. 1d6— roll one 6-sided dice
  3. 2d6— roll two 6-sided dice
  4. 3d6— roll three 6-sided dice
  5. 4d6— roll four 6-sided dice
  6. Automatic Success
  • The highest number rolled determines the roll result

Roll Results

  1. Critical Failure— and something else goes terribly wrong
  2. Standard Failure
  3. Marginal Failure— but not all is lost
  4. Marginal Success— but there's a catch
  5. Standard Success
  6. Critical Success— and something else incredible happens

Other Considerations

Equipment

Player characters are presumed to start with all equipment relevant to their skills. When in doubt, ask the narrator. If equipment is lost, the narrator should determine how it affects the character.

Leveling Up

At the narrators discretion, when a player critically succeeds with a skill, that skill may permanently increase in proficiency. It's recommended to keep this at once per player, per session.

Player Knowledge

Your characters only exist in the world of your adventure. When role-playing them keep your knowledge separate from theirs. They don't necessarily know they failed to scan a room for traps.

6° of State

In lieu of tracking exact values like points or distance, MURPS is meant to abstract the top level states that actually affect situations. For example, the degrees of proficiency and difficulty.
